# Spread and gather are complements
df <- data.frame(x = c("a", "b"), y = c(3, 4), z = c(5, 6))
df
##   x y z
## 1 a 3 5
## 2 b 4 6
df %>% spread(x, y)
##   z  a  b
## 1 5  3 NA
## 2 6 NA  4
df %>% spread(x, y) %>% gather(x, y, a:b, na.rm = TRUE)
##   z x y
## 1 5 a 3
## 4 6 b 4

fill {tidyr} Fills missing values in using the previous entry. This is useful in the common output format where values are not repeated, they’re recorded each time they change.

spread {tidyr} Spread a key-value pair across multiple columns.

complete {tidyr} Turns implicit missing values into explicit missing values. This is a wrapper around expand(), left_join() and replace_na that’s useful for completing missing combinations of data.
